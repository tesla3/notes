## The Macro Picture (2026)

The post-2020 admissions regime ended in 2024–2026. Five structural shifts converged:

1. **Testing came back at the top.** Six of eight Ivies + MIT + Caltech + Stanford + Duke now require SAT/ACT for the 2026–2027 cycle. Only Columbia (permanent) and Princeton (through 2026–27) remain test-optional in the Ivy League.
2. **SFFA killed race-conscious admissions** (June 2023). Two cycles in: Black enrollment dropped sharply at most elites; Asian-American enrollment rose materially at MIT (40% → 47%).
3. **California banned legacy** (AB 1780, effective Sep 2025). Stanford and USC most affected.
4. **Generative AI broke the essay layer.** Common App treats AI-written essays as fraud. Caltech is using AI voice agents to interview applicants.
5. **Application volume detonated.** Common App: 7.6M+ submissions through Jan 1, 2026 (on track to exceed 8M end of cycle). Ivy admit rates compressed below 5% across the board.
6. **Financial aid expansion at top privates** (HYPSM-tier free tuition <$200K–$250K) often makes elite privates *cheaper* than UCs for middle-income families.
7. **Federal sibling discount eliminated** in the new FAFSA SAI (2024–25). CSS Profile schools may compensate institutionally; UCs do not. Direct dollar implication for our overlap years.
