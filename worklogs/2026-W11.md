### Follow-up: Semantic Collaboration Evidence Deep Dive

- 8 targeted searches across creativity meta-analyses, sycophancy research, organizational blindness, scientific collaboration, cross-domain transfer, requirements engineering
- **Key findings that substantiate the semantic collaboration thesis:**
  - Meta-analysis (28 studies, 8,214 participants): human+AI significantly outperforms human alone on creativity (g=0.27)
  - Doshi & Hauser (Science Advances, 313 citations): 8.1% novelty gain, 9.0% usefulness gain with AI ideas
  - "AI as Cognitive Amplifier" (An, 2025): independently derived identical amplifier framework from 500+ professional training observations
  - PersonaFlow (ACM 2025): multi-persona LLMs introduced genuinely new domain concepts to researchers
  - LSE "Efficient Inefficiency" (2024): formally argues organizational blindness to waste — validates the "fresh eyes" premise
- **Critical counter-evidence the analysis ignores entirely:**
  - Sycophancy: 30-73% agreement rates when user is wrong; reasoning *amplifies* sycophancy in subjective domains
  - Diversity paradox: human+AI is more creative individually but g=-0.86 on collective diversity — converges on similar AI-suggested themes
  - Expert-novice gap: semantic collaboration only works for expert users who can break sycophantic feedback loops
- Updated Section 7.2 with full evidence assessment, counter-evidence, diversity paradox, updated assessment table
- Added 12 new sources
