| Evidence | What It Shows |
|---|---|

| PRS middle 50% SAT: 1150-1450 | 300-point range — not uniformly high-performing |

**The core question:** If PRS's Harkness model were driving verbal development, where's the evidence after 2+ years? She's getting A+ on below-level content. The structure exists but the content may not challenge her.

### The A+ Masking Problem
PRS uses "final mastery" grading (year-end grade only). Combined with below-level content, Avery receives perfect grades in her weakest area. The A+ tells everyone — student, parents, school — that English is handled. The 77th percentile MAP says otherwise.

**PRS accelerated her 2 years in math and a level in Spanish when it saw she was ahead. PRS has NOT done this in English.** The question for PRS: "You differentiated in math and Spanish. Why not in English?"

### Key Caveat
Home books ≠ confirmed class assignments. Actual reading list pending from Scott Lyman (English dept chair). This data point is decision-critical. Also: middle school (6-8) may use Harkness differently than upper school (9-12).

### Revised Position
PRS is the better *platform* (structure is right) but may have the wrong *content calibration.* The fix isn't "leave PRS" — it's "raise the bar within PRS." But if PRS can't/won't raise the bar, the $47K/year case collapses.

## Round 5: "CCA Parents Optimize Easy APs — It's Toxic"

### Parent's Claim
Many CCA parents have kids take easy AP courses to optimize GPA. Called this "toxic."

### Pushback Delivered
1. **This isn't CCA-specific.** Happens at every affluent competitive public school in California. Torrey Pines, Gunn, Lynbrook — universal response to UC weighted GPA incentives.
2. **The "toxic" label was challenged:** The parent's own data-driven optimization (Lexile analysis, MAP percentiles, counselor ratios, SAT projections) uses similar methodology to CCA parents gaming APs — different objective function but similar analytical approach.
3. **Easy-AP strategy is self-defeating anyway:** UC GPA caps at 8 semesters of bonus points — stacking 12 APs doesn't help UC GPA beyond 8. Holistic-review schools see through easy AP loading. The families doing it are hurting their kids at exactly the schools Avery targets.
4. **The useful kernel:** Not "CCA is toxic" but "CCA gives Avery maximum freedom to optimize, and a system-optimizer given maximum freedom will optimize for the wrong things."

### Parent's Counter-Pushback
"Disagree. We intended to have her grow better — speak better, reflect deeper, get properly challenged. That's different from optimizing GPA."

### Concession
**Parent was right.** There IS a meaningful difference between:
- "How do I inflate my kid's GPA?" (credential optimization)
- "How do I get my kid to speak better, think deeper, and get genuinely challenged?" (growth optimization)

The analysis methodology looks similar but the objective function is different. CCA parent picks AP Enviro over AP Physics C for GPA bump. This parent spends $189K on PRS for counselor ratio and Harkness because they want genuine verbal development. Not the same thing. Conceded.

### Redirected Advice
"Stop diagnosing CCA and start auditing PRS — the school you're actually paying for, on the thing you're actually paying for it to do."

## Round 6: The Breakthrough — Fixed Mindset + Immediate Feedback

### Parent's Final Revelation
- Avery is **not blind to her weakness** — she knows about ISEE 6/7
- She **refused Speech & Debate** during CCA course selection
- She **"wants to maximize strength and avoid tackling weakness"**
- Since very young: **"I am good at this" / "I am not good at that"** — fixed categories
- Multiple **growth mindset interventions from parents and teachers haven't stuck**
- She has practiced **piano since 5** and done **math since 1st grade** — CAN do sustained long-term development
- She's **"more triggered by immediate feedback rather than delayed one"**

### The Core Diagnosis

**Avery has a fixed mindset about her abilities, reinforced by a preference for immediate feedback, that causes her to maximize strength and systematically avoid weakness.**

This explains everything:

| Behavior | Root Cause |
|---|---|
| 99th percentile math | "I'm good at this" + immediate feedback (right/wrong per problem) → sustained effort |
| Piano since age 5 | "I'm good at this" + immediate feedback (sounds right/wrong) → sustained effort |
| 77th percentile verbal, stalling | "I'm not good at this" + delayed feedback (months to see improvement) → avoidance |
| Refused Speech & Debate at CCA | Public verbal performance = immediate NEGATIVE feedback in weak area → maximum threat |
| Garage FTC over PRS robotics | Minimal contribution = no exposure of weakness → safe |
| "Mini-required, be done with it" | Minimize time in uncomfortable areas → reallocate to strengths |
| A+ in English | Confirms "English is handled" → no need to push harder → weakness persists invisibly |

**She doesn't avoid hard work. She avoids hard work in areas where she believes she's not good.**

### Why Growth Mindset Messaging Failed
Her fixed mindset is **evidence-based from her perspective.** Math 99th ✓ = "I'm good." Verbal 77th, ISEE 6/7 ✓ = "I'm not good." Telling her "you can grow" doesn't override her data. **The only intervention that works is the experience of actual, measurable improvement in the weak area after sustained effort.**

### Why PRS's Grade-Level Projects Are THE Intervention

The 9th Grade Oration is the most important event in Avery's next four years:
1. She'll dread it (verbal = "I'm not good at this")
2. She'll prepare intensely (she always does on clear assignments)
3. She'll deliver (she always delivers)
4. She'll get **immediate audience feedback** — applause, reactions
5. She'll experience success in her "I'm not good at this" domain
6. **Fixed mindset cracks.** Not through messaging. Through experience.

No version of CCA produces this moment. She already refused Speech & Debate.

### Actionable Interventions Designed for Her Wiring

Since she's wired for immediate feedback, design verbal development around that:

1. **Vocabulary.com / Anki** — adaptive, immediate right/wrong, visible streaks/scores. Matches math/piano wiring.
2. **Timed reading comprehension with scoring** — SAT-style passages, timer, score. Number goes up week over week. Fixed mindset can't argue with rising graph.
3. **Writing with rapid feedback** — paragraph → 24-hour feedback → revise → see improvement between drafts. Tight loops.
4. **MAP score tracking** — show her the delta. "77th → 82nd. You did that."
5. **Piano analogy** — "Remember when you couldn't play [piece]? Reading is the same process. You can't hear improvement daily, but the score shows it."
