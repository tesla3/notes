From basic physics, the farther your target, the faster you need to launch—that's roughly a linear relationship. But air drag complicates things: drag force grows with the square of speed, introducing a quadratic term that dominates at higher velocities. So we predicted the optimal launch speed should follow a second-degree polynomial in distance. Our data confirmed this—when we tested polynomials from degree 1 to 5 using cross-validation (a standard technique to avoid overfitting), degree 2 gave the lowest error.
