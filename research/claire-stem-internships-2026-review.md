## Errors Found (Severity-Ranked)

### 🔴 CRITICAL: Salk Institute Deadline is PAST

The research states the Salk Heithoff-Brody deadline is **March 28, 2026**. This is wrong. The actual 2026 deadline was **March 1, 2026** — already past by a week when the research was written. The "March 28" figure was the *2025 extended deadline*, likely scraped from an old application page that hadn't been updated.

- **Source:** Salk's own 2026 UKG application page: "The deadline for Applications is March 1, 2026, at 11:59 p.m."
- **Impact:** The Salk program — listed as a top San Diego option — is not available. The research's timeline section ("March 11-22: Apply to Salk") is giving actionable advice on an already-closed program.

### 🔴 CRITICAL: Scripps REACH is NOT Available to Claire

The research presents Scripps as a general San Diego option with an "April 4, 2026" deadline. Both claims are wrong:

1. **Deadline is March 22, 2026**, not April 4.
2. **Only students from 9 specific REACH partner schools are eligible.** These are: Barrio Logan College Institute, Crawford HS, Del Lago Academy, Escondido HS, Hoover HS, Lincoln HS, Madison HS, Morse HS, Orange Glen HS — all targeting underserved communities.
3. Unless Claire attends one of these schools, **this program does not exist for her.**
4. The research's suggestion to "contact admissions about Honor Chemistry substitution" is moot — the barrier is school enrollment, not coursework.
5. **Stipend is $5,040**, not $4,830 as stated.

### 🔴 CRITICAL: SPARK Deadline Appears Fabricated

The research states SPARK's deadline is "March 28, 2026." No source confirms this. The SPARK program (CIRM-funded, through Sanford Burnham Prebys) has no publicly listed 2026 application deadline in search results. The "March 28" date appears copy-pasted from the erroneous Salk deadline above, suggesting a pattern of date confusion during research.

Additionally, SPARK is explicitly a DEI initiative targeting "high school students without access to summer research internship opportunities due to socioeconomic constraints" (CIRM description). The research notes this but doesn't assess whether Claire qualifies.

### 🟡 MODERATE: Cooper Union Deadline Conflicting

The research says March 22, 2026. Cooper Union's own website shows **two different dates**:
- FAQ/application page: "Application Closes: March 22, 2026 at 11:59 PM"
- Events page: "Deadline to apply: March 27, 2026, 11:59pm EST"

The March 22 date is likely the original deadline; March 27 may be an extension or a different page's update. **Recommend treating March 22 as the safe deadline** — but this needs to be verified directly with Cooper Union (`stem@cooper.edu`) immediately.

### 🟡 MODERATE: Salk Acceptance Rate Unverified

The research claims "~12 students from 250 applicants (4.8% acceptance rate)." No source confirms these numbers. The Salk website says only "a select group" and "high volume of applications." The 4.8% figure may be fabricated or extrapolated without basis. This matters because it would affect strategic prioritization decisions.

### 🟡 MODERATE: Salk Eligibility More Flexible Than Stated

The research states chemistry is required and marks Claire's Honor Chem with ✅. But it also states biology is needed for Scripps and flags Claire hasn't taken it. Salk's own FAQ says: *"If you haven't taken biology or chemistry yet, or are only missing one of those courses, we still encourage you to apply."* This is actually more flexible than stated — but it's moot since the deadline has passed.

### 🟢 MINOR: Cooper Union Class Size

Research says "18-22 students." FAQ says "20-25 students per class." Minor, but indicative of the pattern: numbers in this document are not reliably sourced.

## Meta-Observation: How These Errors Happened

The error pattern is diagnostic:
- **Date confusion:** The March 28 "deadline" for Salk was the 2025 extended deadline, scraped from a page that hadn't been refreshed for 2026. The same phantom date appears for SPARK. When researching time-sensitive information, the year on the source page must be verified — not just the date.
- **Eligibility blindness:** Scripps REACH's partner-school restriction is on the exact same page as the deadline. The research extracted the deadline and requirements but missed the most disqualifying criterion — the school enrollment restriction — likely because it was formatted as a bold notice above the requirements section rather than within them.
- **Precision fabrication:** Specific numbers (12 students, 250 applicants, $4,830 stipend, 18-22 students) create false confidence. When exact numbers can't be sourced, the honest move is to say "not publicly disclosed" rather than present estimates as facts.

**Lesson:** For decision-critical research with real deadlines affecting a real person, every deadline and eligibility criterion must be verified against the program's current official page, with the year explicitly confirmed. Secondary sources and cached pages are not sufficient.
