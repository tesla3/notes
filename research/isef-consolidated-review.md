### GAP 5: The Document Doesn't Explain What Makes an ML/CS Project ISEF-Competitive

The document identifies "ML fatigue" but doesn't synthesize a workable strategy for a student whose strength IS ML/CS. The winning formula from 2024-2025:

**What ML/CS projects that win do differently:**
1. ML is the TOOL, not the subject — it solves a specific, concrete, measurable problem
2. The problem has clear human impact (health, safety, accessibility, cost)
3. There's domain expertise beyond the ML (understanding the problem space deeply)
4. Results are validated against non-ML baselines (shows the ML adds value, not just novelty)
5. The student can explain model architecture choices, not just hyperparameter tuning

**Anti-pattern (what loses):**
- "I applied [model X] to [dataset Y] and got [accuracy Z]" — this is a tutorial, not research
- Black-box application with no mechanistic understanding
- No comparison to simpler methods
- Problem chosen because data was available, not because it matters

**For Claire's Roblox × ML candidates specifically:**
- Safety Guardrail Benchmark: Could work IF framed as "I systematically evaluated AND FOUND specific failure modes that create real child safety risks" — the contribution is the vulnerability discovery, ML is the probe
- Math Model Benchmark: Strongest because it's genuinely novel ("Where do frontier reasoning models fail on AP Calc BC, and WHY?") — the contribution is understanding failure modes of AI, which is timely, original, and doesn't require ML building, just systematic evaluation

### GAP 8: Team vs. Individual Trade-offs

The document never addresses whether Claire should compete individually or as a team (2-3 members). Critical considerations:

- **Team projects compete in the same categories** with same award amounts
- Team members share awards (financial split)
- A Grand Award winner team = each member becomes an "ISEF Grand Award winner" (equally valid for admissions)
- Teams can tackle more ambitious projects (more execution capacity)
- BUT: judges assess each member's individual contribution — "What did YOU do?" is asked of each
- A weak team member drags down interview scores
- For admissions signal: individual winners are slightly stronger (no ambiguity about who did what)

**Recommendation:** Individual unless there's a true complementary partnership (e.g., one person does hardware, one does software). Solo ownership is cleaner for interviews and admissions narrative.

## Part 7: Optimal Strategy Recommendations (Additions)

### For an ML/CS-Strong Student at Bishop's School, GSDSEF Path

**Tier 1 — Highest EV moves (do immediately):**

1. **Connect with Kayley Xu** — same school, same competition, same tech area (AI/ML + health). She's the closest-possible mentor with actual ISEF Grand Award experience.

2. **Identify UCSD summer research program** — REHS (requires age 16+), BE-SHIP, OPALS, or Research Scholars. Apply for summer 2026 deadlines (some may have passed; others rolling). This gives: mentor, lab notebook, institutional legitimacy, Form 1C clarity.

3. **Choose Systems Software (SOFT) or Mathematics (MATH)** — NOT Computational Biology, NOT Biomedical Engineering, NOT Translational Medical Science. These are the less saturated categories where an ML-adjacent project has breathing room.

**Tier 2 — Project framing (before summer):**

4. **"Math Model Benchmark" (AP Calc) is the strongest ISEF candidate** of the three options listed. Why:
   - Category: Mathematics (less saturated, judges are domain experts who WON'T have ML fatigue)
   - Research question: "Where and how do frontier reasoning models systematically fail on rigorous mathematical reasoning?" — timely, novel, testable
   - Quantified impact: "Models X, Y, Z fail on [specific problem types] with [N]% error rates despite claiming [capability]"
   - No ML fatigue: you're EVALUATING AI, not building another model. Judges see this as meta-science, not "I trained a model."
   - Low resource requirement: no lab, no expensive equipment, home-executable
   - Form 1C: not needed (no institutional lab required)

5. **Safety Guardrail Benchmark** is second-best IF reframed:
   - NOT: "I tested AI safety guardrails on Roblox"
   - YES: "I developed a systematic methodology for evaluating content moderation systems and discovered [specific, quantified] failure modes that affect [N million] child users"
   - Category: Systems Software (SOFT) or Behavioral and Social Sciences (BEHA)
   - Risk: "Roblox" in the title makes it sound less serious. Frame around child safety, not gaming.

**Tier 3 — Timeline optimization:**

6. **GSDSEF 2027 registration opens ~Fall 2026.** Research should be in execution phase by September 2026.
7. **SRC (Scientific Review Committee) approval needed before experiments.** For computational/software projects this is simpler than wet-lab work but still required.
8. **Plan for BOTH GSDSEF (March 2027) AND CSEF (April 2027)** — GSDSEF Grand Award winners go directly to ISEF; others go to CSEF with a second chance (6 CSEF→ISEF spots statewide).
