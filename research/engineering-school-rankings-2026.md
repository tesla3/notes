Compiled May 15, 2026. Research done for TBS Class of 2026 college decisions analysis.

## Recommended Ranking: US News 2026 Best Graduate Engineering Schools

**Why this one:** The single most authoritative engineering school ranking in the US. Multi-factor methodology (peer assessment, research output, student selectivity, faculty resources). Referenced by engineering faculty, employers, and grad schools. Unlike THE (which puts Harvard #1 for eng — absurd for undergrad context) or the US News UG Engineering ranking (peer-assessment-only, paywalled beyond top 26), this ranking is verifiable, consistent, and intuitive.

## Other Rankings Considered (and why they're worse)

### US News 2026 Best Undergraduate Engineering Programs (Doctorate)

- **Methodology:** Peer-assessment survey only (engineering deans and senior faculty rate programs).
- **Pros:** Most commonly cited for US undergrad admissions context. Published by the gold-standard source.
- **Cons:** Paywalled beyond top 26. Peer-assessment-only means ~25% response rate and no objective data. Rankings beyond top 10 are very noisy.

### Times Higher Education (THE) 2026 Subject Rankings: Engineering

- **Methodology:** Teaching (30%), research environment (30%), research quality (30%), industry (4%), international outlook (7.5%).
- **Pros:** Covers 144 US schools. Full data publicly available.
- **Cons:** Heavily research-weighted → Harvard #1 for engineering (makes no sense for undergrad). Ranks entire university, not just engineering school. Georgia Tech at #7 (should be higher). Rankings don't match how the engineering world perceives these programs.

- Less authoritative than US News. Useful as a cross-reference only.

## Key Takeaways

1. **US News Grad Engineering** is the best single ranking: consistent source, multi-factor, matches real-world perception.
2. **US News UG Engineering** would be ideal for undergrad context but is paywalled and peer-assessment-only.
3. **THE is misleading** for undergrad engineering — research-weight distorts results (Harvard #1, Georgia Tech #7).
4. For schools not in any engineering ranking: UChicago (no eng school), Boston College, Emory, Georgetown, Babson, LACs — these are strong schools that simply don't offer engineering.
5. Cal Poly SLO is ranked on a separate US News list (non-doctorate) where it's consistently #1–2.
