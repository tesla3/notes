## Key Findings

### The Competitive Reality
My initial research revealed a **two-tier competitive landscape**:

**Tier 1: Nationally Elite** (3-10% acceptance rates)
- **SEAP:** 3-13% acceptance rate. 938 applications for 28 spots (3%) in some years. 200+ applications for 20 spots at single labs.
- **JCVI:** ~3.6% acceptance rate (550 applications for 20 spots). Student with 2380 SAT, Caltech admission, Chemistry Olympiad top 150 nationally was **rejected**.
- **NASA flagship programs:** "Often below 10%" acceptance rate. SEES specifically 3-4%.
- **Requirements:** National-level science competitions, prior research experience, exceptional standardized scores, science fair awards.

**Tier 2: Regional/Accessible** (20-50% acceptance rates)  
- University-based programs, regional NASA programs, industry partnerships
- Focus on potential over achievements
- Strong academics sufficient (like Claire's profile)

**Cooper Union Summer STEM** ⭐ **TOP PRIORITY**
- **Deadline:** March 22, 2026 (14 days from research date)
- **Perfect fit:** 10th grade eligible, college-level engineering courses
- **Cost:** $3,150 (3-week) / $5,150 (6-week)
- **Financial aid:** Full/partial for families under NYC median income
