# Elite STEM Training: Core Findings

Quick-reference summary of `lac-stem-decision-framework.md`. May 2026.

---

## 1. The Sorting Question: Natural Science vs. Engineering

Everything flows from this. The evidence points in opposite directions depending on the answer.

**Natural science PhD path → LACs are strongest per capita.**
- NSF census data (60+ years): LACs produce S&E PhDs at higher per-capita rates than R1s (NSF 22-321, 2022)
- Swarthmore: 481 PhDs/1,000 undergrads. MIT: 487. Harvey Mudd: 509. Caltech: 891.
- No public university in the per-capita top 25
- Mechanism: undergraduate research as junior colleague, not lab assistant (*J. Phys. Chem. C*, 2025)

**Engineering path → LACs are structurally disqualified (with two exceptions).**
- Most LACs have no engineering program. 3-2 workarounds are rarely used (<5/class)
- Only exceptions: Swarthmore (ABET-accredited), Harvey Mudd (STEM-focused)
- For engineering: MIT ≈ Stanford > GT ≈ Berkeley ≈ Michigan > Harvey Mudd > Cornell > Swarthmore
- NSF 08-311: LACs have "far lower yield in engineering than the private research universities"

---

## 2. The PhD Pipeline Is Tightening — This Helps LACs

- Harvard cut science PhD slots 75%, humanities 60% (2025–26)
- Chicago, Yale, Brown pausing or cutting PhD admissions
- Cuts are supply-side (endowment taxes, unionization costs), not demand-side
- AI increases societal need for frontier researchers while institutions contract slots
- **Fewer slots → stronger competition → preparation quality matters more → LAC mentorship advantage concentrates**
- Strongest in: natural sciences, computational biology, materials science, energy
- Individual timing risk is real: job market in 2031–33 is genuinely uncertain

## 3. Claremont 5C Is the Strongest Structural Position for STEM + Breadth

The consortium uniquely eliminates the three core LAC weaknesses:

| LAC weakness | Claremont fix |
|---|---|
| No engineering | Harvey Mudd (509 PhDs/1,000; ABET-accredited) |
| Limited courses | 2,700+ courses across 5 colleges |
| Small social world | 6,000+ undergrads, each college with distinct culture |

- Contiguous campuses — 20-minute walk end-to-end, seamless cross-registration
- Pomona student can take engineering at HMC, policy at CMC, humanities at Scripps
- 35 miles from Caltech, JPL, UCLA for summer research access
- No other LAC cluster matches this combination

## 4. Cost Inverts by Income — The Counterintuitive Finding

| Family Income | Elite Private (net) | State Flagship (in-state) | Who wins |
|---|---|---|---|
| <$75K | $0–$10K | $10K–$20K | **Elite private is cheaper** |
| $75K–$150K | $10K–$35K | $15K–$30K | Roughly comparable |
| $150K–$300K | $35K–$73K | $20K–$35K | **Flagship wins by $15K–$40K/yr** |
| >$400K | ~$85K–$92K | $20K–$35K | **Flagship wins by $50K–$60K/yr** |

Source: Bloomberg (April 2025); Bowdoin net price table; Williams Provost (2023–24).

- Families <$75K should not let sticker price deter elite private applications
- Families $150K–$300K face the real squeeze: $120K–$200K extra over 4 years
- State flagships offer merit scholarships; elite privates do not (need-based only)
- STEM-specific: well-endowed privates fund summer research stipends at higher per-capita rates

---

## 5. Selectivity Effects Are Real — But Not for Everyone

- **Dale-Krueger** (*QJE* 2002, *JHR* 2014): no earnings premium from selectivity after controlling for student quality — **except** for Black, Hispanic students
- **Chetty** (*QJE* 2023): causal effects of elite attendance on top-1% income, elite grad school, prestigious firms — with more recent data and stronger methodology
- **Ge/Isaac/Miller** (*JPE* 2022): selectivity boosts women's earnings 14% and advanced degree attainment 5 percentage points. No effect for men.

**Resolution — it's heterogeneity, not contradiction:**
- Selectivity matters most for: low-income, first-gen, URM, and women
- Selectivity matters least for: upper-middle-class white men from connected families
- Implication: the students who benefit most from elite attendance are least likely to be admitted

---

## 6. Institution Type Doesn't Predict Wellbeing — Experiences Do

- Gallup-Purdue (2014, 30,000+ graduates): "The type of schools these college graduates attended — public or private, small or large, very selective or less selective — hardly matters at all to their workplace engagement and current well-being"
- What matters: a professor who cared, made learning exciting, encouraged dreams (2x engagement); internship + long-term project + deep extracurriculars (2x thriving)
- Only 3% of graduates had all six experiences — at any institution type
- LACs are structurally positioned to deliver these but don't guarantee them
- A motivated student at Michigan Honors can build equivalent experiences

## 7. The Decision Matrix

| Student profile | Best path | Why |
|---|---|---|
| PhD-bound in natural science | Swarthmore / Carleton / Reed / Caltech / HMC | Per-capita PhD data; mentorship mechanism |
| Engineering career or PhD | MIT / Stanford / GT / Michigan / Berkeley | Infrastructure, ABET, industry pipelines |
| STEM + breadth + optionality | Claremont 5C or MIT or Princeton | Consortium breadth; no structural weaknesses |
| Cost-constrained, $150K–$300K family | State flagship honors (in-state) | $25K/yr, world-class programs, wide optionality |
| Low-income (<$75K), any STEM field | Most selective school admitted to, with full aid | Chetty: causal effect is largest here; may be cheaper than flagship |
| Undecided, might switch to engineering | Flagship or Claremont 5C | Can't switch to engineering at Williams |
| Woman in STEM | Most selective institution admitted to | *JPE* 2022: 14% earnings boost, 5pp advanced degree boost |

---

## 8. The Bottom Line

**AI automates execution. The durable premium is frontier research — formulating questions, not running procedures.**

- LACs train for this better per capita than any other model (NSF data, 60+ years)
- But they can't train engineers (except Swarthmore, Harvey Mudd)
- Claremont 5C is the only model that combines LAC mentorship + engineering + breadth
- State flagships deliver 80% of the outcome at 30% of the cost — for self-directed students
- The right answer depends on: what field, what family income, how self-directed, how certain

**The question is not which model is "best." It's which matches your student's temperament, your family's finances, and the specific bet you're making about what the future rewards.**
