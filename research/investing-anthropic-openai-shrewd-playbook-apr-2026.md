## III. The Real Plays: Public Companies With Hidden AI Lab Equity

This is where the shrewd money goes. The market is efficient at pricing VCX (everyone knows the holdings). It is **less efficient** at pricing AI lab equity embedded inside large, diversified public companies — though this gap is narrowing as sell-side analysts increasingly model these stakes explicitly (Morningstar, Rijnberk Invest Insights, and others now publish sum-of-parts analyses for GOOGL including Anthropic/SpaceX/Waymo).

### Play #1: Alphabet (GOOGL) — THE Position ⭐⭐⭐

**The thesis:** Alphabet is the only entity that wins regardless of which AI lab dominates. It controls the full stack — frontier model (Gemini), custom silicon (TPU), hyperscale cloud, distribution (Search, Workspace, Android), AND equity stakes in the competition.

**The hidden assets:**

*Note: These are minority stakes with no board seats (Anthropic) or limited governance. Google cannot freely sell during IPO lock-ups (typically 180 days), and block sales of 7–14% stakes incur 3–7% discounts. A standard 15–25% illiquidity/minority discount should be applied. The figures below are gross values before such discounts.*

| Asset | Gross Value | Discounted Value (est. 20% haircut) | % of GOOGL market cap (~$3.4T) |
|-------|-------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 14% Anthropic stake (at $380B) | ~$53B | ~$42B | 1.2% |
| 14% Anthropic stake (at IPO, est. $500–700B) | ~$70–98B | ~$56–78B | 1.6–2.3% |
| ~7% SpaceX stake (at ~$1T) | ~$70B | ~$56B | 1.6% |
| ~7% SpaceX stake (at IPO, est. $1–1.5T) | ~$70–105B | ~$56–84B | 1.6–2.5% |
| Waymo (at $126B funding valuation) | ~$126B (100% owned) | ~$126B (no discount — wholly owned) | 3.7% |
| **Total hidden equity** | **$193–329B** | **~$154–288B** | **4.5–8.5%** |

Anthropic and SpaceX IPOs in the next 6–12 months will force the market to price these. Waymo's 2026 expansion (20+ cities, London, 1M trips/week target) will force a revaluation too.

**The triple-play moat** (Morningstar's framework):
- If Gemini wins → Alphabet wins directly
- If Claude wins → Alphabet wins via 14% equity + multi-billion dollar Cloud contract ($8–10B/year to run Anthropic's training)
- If both win → Alphabet wins twice
- No other company has this structure.

**The Cloud business alone:**
- Growing 48% YoY, $17.7B in Q4 2025
- Anthropic's cloud contract (TPU-based) contributes $8–10B/year
- Cloud is now profitable and accelerating

**Valuation:** ~25x forward earnings. Not cheap, but not stretched for a company growing revenue 12% with Cloud growing 48% and $154–288B in hidden equity (after illiquidity discount, representing 4.5–8.5% of the ~$3.4T market cap).

**Risk:** Gemini execution (3.1 Pro is benchmark-leading but Google has historically fumbled product launches), antitrust (DOJ search monopoly ruling), macro slowdown. *Commoditization risk also applies here: if frontier AI models become commodity-priced, Gemini's differentiation erodes and the Anthropic stake loses value for the same reason — the "triple play" thesis works best if frontier models retain pricing power.*

**Entry:** Current market weakness (Nasdaq in correction) provides a better entry than January highs. The Iran war selloff is creating a broad-based discount on quality tech.

### Play #2: Zoom (ZM) — The Asymmetric Value Play ⭐⭐⭐

**The thesis:** The market prices Zoom as a declining video conferencing company. It's actually a cash fortress with a hidden $2–4B+ AI option that the market hasn't fully repriced.

*Important caveat: Zoom invested $51M in Anthropic in 2023. After $50B+ in subsequent Anthropic funding rounds, the exact ownership percentage is unknown — estimated at 0.6–1.1% depending on anti-dilution protections (which are not publicly disclosed). Wall Street analysts (Seeking Alpha, Filmogaz) estimate the stake at $2–4B. The math below uses this range.*

**The math:**

| Component | Value (conservative) | Value (optimistic) |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------|
| Market cap | ~$23B | ~$23B |
| Cash & investments | $7.8B (34% of market cap) | $7.8B (34% of market cap) |
| Debt | $0 | $0 |
| Anthropic stake (~0.6% at $380B) | ~$2.3B (10% of market cap) | — |
| Anthropic stake (~1.1% at $380B) | — | ~$4.2B (18% of market cap) |
| Anthropic stake (at IPO, $500–700B) | ~$3.0B (0.6%) | ~$7.7B (1.1%) |
| **Cash + Anthropic = floor** | **$10.1–10.8B (44–47% of market cap)** | **$12.0–15.5B (52–67% of market cap)** |
| **Implied value of core business** | **$12.9B** | **$7.5–11.0B** |
| Core business FCF | ~$1.7–2.0B/year | ~$1.7–2.0B/year |
| **Implied FCF multiple on core** | **~6.5–7.6x** | **~3.8–6.5x** |

Even in the conservative case, you're getting the core business at 6–8x FCF, well below the S&P 500 average of ~18x FCF. In the optimistic case, it's a deep value play.

**What breaks it higher:**
1. **Anthropic IPO (October 2026):** Forces mark-to-market of the stake. Analysts will have to revise sum-of-parts models. *However: the IPO forces an accounting event, not a cash event — Zoom cannot sell Anthropic shares during the standard 180-day lock-up period. The repricing is gradual, not instant.*
2. **CX business acceleration:** Zoom Customer Experience grew high-double-digits in Q4, with paid AI in all top 10 deals. If this leg grows, the "declining company" narrative breaks.
3. **Capital returns:** $7.8B cash with no debt = buyback potential, which directly boosts EPS at these prices. *Note: Management has not announced buybacks at current levels. Without an explicit capital return program or activist pressure, the cash "floor" is theoretical — a common characteristic of value traps.*

**What breaks it lower:**
- Enterprise net dollar expansion fell below 100% — existing customers are spending less
- Online churn is elevated
- Core video is commoditized (Microsoft Teams, Google Meet are free)
- Q4 free cash flow declined 19% YoY

**Risk management:** The $7.8B cash provides a theoretical floor. Even in a bear case where the core business slowly declines, the cash + Anthropic stake protects against catastrophic downside. Zoom won't go to zero — the liquidation value is $10.1B+ against a $23B market cap. *However: without an activist or management-initiated capital return, cash-rich companies with declining cores can trade below liquidation value for extended periods (see: IBM 2013–2020). The floor only works if someone forces the unlock.*

**Key risk:** The Anthropic stake is carried at cost (~$51M) on the balance sheet. If Anthropic's IPO is delayed or prices lower than expected, the optionality premium shrinks. The ownership percentage itself is uncertain (0.6–1.1% estimated) because anti-dilution protections are not publicly disclosed, and Anthropic has raised $50B+ since Zoom's investment. The actual stake could be at the low end of this range.

**Entry:** Current price ~$80. Post-earnings dip (-12%) creates a reasonable entry. If it falls further in the macro selloff, even better.

### Play #3: Microsoft (MSFT) — Best OpenAI Proxy, But Wait ⭐⭐

**The thesis:** 27% OpenAI equity worth ~$135–230B. Azure growing 39% with OpenAI contributing ~10 points. $250B in committed OpenAI cloud spend. The deepest integration with the leading consumer AI product.

**Why wait:**
1. **Musk v. OpenAI trial starts April 27.** Jury selection in Oakland, presided over by Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers (who handled Epic v. Apple). **Musk seeks $134B in total damages** alleging contract fraud and breach of fiduciary duty. Judge Rogers called some calculations "made up" but ruled the questions substantial enough for a jury. While the probability of a full $134B award is very low, even a partial verdict ($5–15B) could hit MSFT sentiment given the 27% equity stake and concentrated RPO exposure.
2. **OpenAI exclusivity eroding.** AWS ($38B deal), Oracle ($300B commitment). The "Azure-exclusive" moat is gone.
3. **OpenAI concentration risk.** OpenAI accounts for ~45% of Microsoft's commercial remaining performance obligations. If OpenAI's burn rate ($14B loss in 2026) creates financial stress before IPO, Microsoft takes collateral damage.

**The smart play:** Wait until the Musk trial resolves (late May/early June). Three scenarios:
- Musk loses entirely → relief rally, MSFT pops, but you can still buy reasonably
- Mixed verdict (partial damages, say $5–15B) → short-term dip, good entry
- Musk wins big ($50B+) → significant dip, potentially excellent entry on the overreaction

The trial outcome is a near-term binary event. Don't front-run it.

### Play #4: Amazon (AMZN) — The Overlooked Anthropic Play ⭐⭐

**The thesis:** Amazon is the largest single investor in Anthropic ($8B total), with an estimated ~7.8% equity stake. Claude is deeply integrated into AWS Bedrock, Anthropic uses Amazon’s Trainium chips for training, and AWS is the world’s largest cloud provider. Yet the Anthropic angle is rarely discussed in AMZN coverage because it’s dwarfed by the core retail and AWS businesses.

**The math:**
- ~7.8% Anthropic stake at $380B = ~$29.6B
- At IPO ($500–700B) = ~$39–55B
- Against AMZN’s ~$2.1T market cap: 1.4–2.6%
- AWS also earns Bedrock distribution revenue on top of the equity stake

**Why it’s less discussed:** The Anthropic stake is a smaller percentage of Amazon’s market cap than Google’s, and Amazon’s investment narrative is dominated by AWS growth and retail margins. But in absolute dollars, AMZN holds more Anthropic equity than Zoom by a factor of 7–15x.

**Why it’s not the top play:** Amazon’s Anthropic exposure is diluted by the company’s enormous market cap. The stake moves the needle less than GOOGL’s 14% or ZM’s cash-plus-Anthropic sum-of-parts. But for investors who want diversified AI lab exposure without single-stock concentration, AMZN provides a safer base case — AWS is growing 19% YoY with or without the Anthropic angle.

**Risk:** Amazon’s $50B commitment to Anthropic bundled with a $100B AWS spending agreement introduces mutual dependency. If Anthropic’s spend doesn’t ramp as projected, the cloud RPO overstates the relationship’s value.

## IV. What NOT To Do

### Don't buy AI labs directly at these valuations

| Company | Valuation | Revenue | P/S (trailing) | P/S (at pricing) | Profitable? | Red Flags |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------------|-------------------|-------------|-----------|
| Anthropic | $380B | ~$19B ARR | ~20x | ~27x (at $14B ARR when Series G priced, Feb 2026) | No (FCF+ 2027, profitable 2028) | Model commoditization, compute costs |
| OpenAI | $850B | ~$29.4B projected | ~29x | ~29x | No (2029–2030) | Musk trial ($134B sought), $14B annual burn, market share declining |

These are priced for perfection in an imperfect world. The commoditization signal is flashing:

- DeepSeek V3.2: $0.14/M tokens
- Claude Sonnet 4.6: $5.00/M tokens
- **36x price gap, quality gap narrowing**
- Developers are already routing 80%+ of tasks to cheap models
- Open-source (Llama 4, 10M token context) closing fast

The labs will respond with safety, reliability, enterprise support. But that's a 2–3 year moat, not a permanent one. At 20–29x trailing revenue (27–29x at time of pricing) for unprofitable companies in a commoditizing market, the margin of safety is zero.

### Don't pay premium-to-NAV on closed-end funds

VCX at 675–1,300%+ premium is the clearest "don't" in this entire analysis. But even DXYZ at 45% premium is paying $1.45 for $1.00 of assets. In a risk-off environment (VIX at 29, Nasdaq in correction, Iran war), premiums compress. The person buying VCX at $280 today could be looking at $80 in a market correction — and the NAV would still be ~$20.

### Don't ignore the macro

The Iran war and oil shock create a backdrop where:
- Risk assets are selling off
- Rates are rising (kills growth stock valuations)
- Inflation is re-emerging (Fed can't cut)
- Quality + cash outperform speculation

This favors: GOOGL (profitable, diversified), ZM (cash fortress), BSTZ (income + discount).
This punishes: VCX (speculative premium), DXYZ (speculative premium), unprofitable companies at high multiples.

## VII. The Uncomfortable Truths

### 1. The best time to invest was 18 months ago.

Anthropic was $60B in January 2025. It's $380B now (6.3x in 14 months). OpenAI was $157B in October 2024. It's $850B now (5.4x in 18 months). The easy money has been made by private investors who got in at venture prices. Retail investors buying at $380B and $850B are buying the institutional exits.

### 2. Revenue growth doesn't mean profitable growth.

Anthropic: $19B ARR, not profitable until 2028. OpenAI: $29.4B projected revenue, not profitable until 2029–2030, burning $14B/year. Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. At current burn rates, both companies need continued massive fundraising or IPO proceeds to survive. The IPO isn't a milestone — it's a lifeline.

### 3. Commoditization is the structural bear case nobody wants to price.

The gap between frontier and near-frontier models is shrinking every quarter. DeepSeek at 36x cheaper, Llama 4 open-source, Xiaomi entering. The Economist's observation stands: "Unlike conventional software companies, which generate more profit the more they scale, AI firms face higher costs the bigger they get." This inverted scaling dynamic breaks every traditional tech valuation model.

### 4. Three mega-IPOs in one cycle is unprecedented.

Anthropic (~$500B+), OpenAI (~$850B+), and SpaceX (~$1–1.5T) are all targeting 2026–2027. Combined: ~$2.5–3T in new public market supply. The market may not have enough capital to absorb all three at target valuations. If one disappoints, sentiment contagion could hit the others.

### 5. The shrewdest move might be the most boring one.

Buy Alphabet. Buy Zoom. Collect some BSTZ income. Keep cash for the IPOs. Don't chase the mania in closed-end fund premiums. Don't pay 675–1,300%+ over NAV. Don't buy secondary shares at inflated prices.

The biggest returns in investing come from buying good assets that are mispriced, not from buying expensive assets that are popular. In April 2026, the mispriced assets are GOOGL (hidden equity the market underweights, though increasingly recognized), ZM (cash box the market treats as a declining video company, though stake size is uncertain), and BSTZ (11% discount with activist catalyst). The popular assets are VCX, DXYZ, and secondary market shares — which is exactly why they're overpriced.

**But neither company is a good investment at current private market prices.** The risk-adjusted path is through public equities with embedded AI lab exposure.

The shrewd investor's edge in April 2026 is **patience and indirection:**
- Patient because the IPOs will provide better entry points with real financial disclosure
- Indirect because GOOGL, ZM, and BSTZ give you AI lab exposure at a discount, with profitable businesses and cash piles as downside protection

**The single best trade right now:** Buy GOOGL on the Iran war dip (down 21% from February highs). You get a ~$3.4T business growing Cloud at 48%, with $154–288B in hidden equity after illiquidity discount (Anthropic + SpaceX + Waymo, ~4.5–8.5% of market cap) that the market will be forced to reprice when the IPOs hit. No premium. No mark-to-model NAV. Just a great business with free options — though these are minority stakes with no governance rights and limited near-term liquidity.

That's what the shrewdest investor does. Not the exciting trade. The correct one.
