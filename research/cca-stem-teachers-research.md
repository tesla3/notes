# Canyon Crest Academy — STEM Teachers & Departments Research

Source: Web research, May 2026. For daughter's school decision.

## School Overview

- Public high school, grades 9–12, Pacific Highlands Ranch / Carmel Valley, San Diego
- Enrollment: ~2,000–2,183 students
- 4×4 block schedule (4 classes/term × 2 terms = 8 classes/year, each 90 min)
- Student-teacher ratio: 26:1 (high; CA avg ~21:1)
- 84 full-time teachers; only 6 in first or second year (~7% — low turnover)
- Niche grade: A+ Teachers, A+ Academics, A+ College Prep
- Ranked top 1% in California for test scores (math proficiency 85%, CA avg 34%)

## Summary Assessment

| Subject | Teaching Quality | Course Depth | Competition Culture | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Math** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Shay) / ⭐⭐ (others) | Excellent (through Linear Algebra) | Strong (AMC/AIME/USAMO) | Teacher lottery |
| **Physics** | ⭐⭐⭐½ (competent) | Good (AP Physics 1, likely C) | Science Olympiad | No standout teacher ID'd |
| **Chemistry** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (student-nominated) | Good (AP Chem) | Science Olympiad + fairs | None found |
| **Biology** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (student-nominated) | Good (AP Bio) | Excellent (ISEF-level) | Pressure → fraud case |
| **Computer Science** | ⭐⭐⭐ (adequate) | AP CSA + CTE | Excellent (FRC Worlds, USACO Platinum) | Depth is peer/club-driven |

**Key insight:** CCA's STEM strength is the **peer ecosystem** (competitions, clubs, research culture) more than uniformly excellent classroom instruction. The best teachers (Shay) are world-class; others are uneven. The 4×4 schedule compresses demanding STEM subjects. Self-directed students thrive; students needing nurturing instruction face risk.
