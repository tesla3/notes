## Deep Dive: Each Vehicle

### 1. Fundrise Innovation Fund (VCX) ⭐ THE PICK for maximum AI lab exposure

**What it is:** The first large-scale "public venture capital" fund to list on a major US exchange. Previously only available through Fundrise's platform with quarterly liquidity; converting to NYSE-listed with daily liquidity starting ~March 9, 2026.

**Holdings (as of 2/15/26):**

| Company | % of Fund |
|---------|-----------|
| **Anthropic** | **21%** |
| Databricks | 18% |
| **OpenAI** | **10%** |
| Anduril | 7% |
| Ramp | 5% |
| SpaceX | 5% |
| Epic Games | 4% |

**Performance:**
- 2026 YTD: +13.43%
- 1-Year: **+63.27%**
- Since Inception (cumulative): +84.4%
- AUM: ~$650M
- Investors: 100,000+

**Structure:** Registered under the Investment Company Act of 1940. Will trade on NYSE like a closed-end fund. No creation/redemption mechanism like an ETF — price determined by supply/demand, can deviate from NAV.

**Why it's the top pick:**
- **31% combined Anthropic + OpenAI** — nothing else comes close
- **$10 minimum, no accreditation** — democratized access
- Strong diversification across top private tech (Databricks, Anduril, SpaceX, Ramp)
- 63% 1-year return demonstrates portfolio quality

**Key risks:**
- **Day-one premium risk.** As a newly listed CEF with massive hype, VCX could trade at 20-50%+ premium to NAV on listing day. DXYZ traded at 500%+ premium at one point. **Do not pay a massive premium.**
- **NAV is marked-to-model.** Private holdings valued internally. If Anthropic/OpenAI IPO at lower-than-expected valuations, NAV resets downward.
- **Concentration.** 39% in Anthropic + Databricks. If Anthropic stumbles, the fund takes a major hit.
- **Fee structure.** Historically ~1.5% management fee. Not cheap, but not DXYZ-level extraction.
- **No ETF arbitrage mechanism.** Unlike AGIX, no authorized participants keeping price ≈ NAV. You're at the mercy of market sentiment.

**Shrewd play:** Wait 2-4 weeks post-listing. Let the FOMO buyers drive the premium, then buy when it settles toward NAV. If it somehow trades at a discount (unlikely near-term but possible in a market correction), that's a gift.

### 2. KraneShares AI & Technology ETF (AGIX) ⭐ THE PICK for cost-conscious, diversified AI exposure

**What it is:** The only publicly listed **ETF** (not CEF) with direct cap-table ownership in Anthropic. Hybrid structure: ~85% tracks the Solactive Etna AGI Index (public AI stocks) + ~15% in select private AI companies.

**Private Holdings (as of recent):**

| Company | % of Fund | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| SpaceX/xAI | 3.2% (combined post-merger) | Was 6.6% xAI pre-merger |
| **Anthropic** | **2.5-2.7%** | 4x appreciation since Feb 2025 investment |

**Key facts:**
- Expense ratio: **0.99%** — cheapest among vehicles with meaningful Anthropic exposure
- Outperformed benchmark by **8.54%** since inception, driven entirely by private holdings
- AUM: relatively small (~$34M as of mid-2025, growing)
- **Direct cap-table position** — no SPV intermediary (unlike XOVR). Better governance, fewer hidden fees.

**Why it's strong:**
- **ETF structure = daily NAV arbitrage.** Authorized participants keep price ≈ NAV. You won't buy at a 50% premium.
- **Cheapest fees** among all private-exposure vehicles
- **Diversified AI ecosystem.** Public sleeve includes Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta, Alphabet, Apple, Broadcom, TSMC, Amazon — the entire AI stack.
- **Transparent** daily holdings disclosure.
- **Intraday liquidity** — buy/sell anytime during market hours.

**Limitations:**
- **Only 2.5-2.7% Anthropic.** You need a large position for meaningful exposure.
- **No OpenAI.** Zero direct exposure.
- **Small fund.** Wider bid-ask spreads than mega-cap ETFs.
- **SEC Rule 22e-4 cap.** Max 15% illiquid assets — limits how much private exposure the fund can hold.

**Shrewd play:** AGIX is the "responsible" choice. You get broad AI sector exposure (the public sleeve is solid) with a private alpha kicker. If Anthropic IPOs and the position reprices upward, the private sleeve drives outperformance. If the AI bubble deflates, the public holdings (Nvidia, MSFT, etc.) provide a floor. Best for a core portfolio allocation, not a speculative bet.

**Why a shrewd investor avoids it:**

**Shrewd take:** DXYZ is a retail speculation vehicle, not an investment. The NAV premium is a tax on ignorance. Hard pass.

## Critical Warning: The "Volatility Laundering" Problem

AQR's Cliff Asness coined this term for private market funds. Because private holdings aren't repriced daily, they **appear** less volatile than they actually are. The 63% 1-year return on VCX looks smooth because Anthropic's valuation only updates when a new funding round or secondary transaction occurs. But if Anthropic were public, the daily volatility would be stomach-churning.

When these companies IPO, the smooth private valuations get replaced by real-time market pricing. Expect:
- Significant NAV volatility in funds holding these companies
- Potential discount-widening in CEFs (VCX, BSTZ) as market prices adjust faster than NAV updates
- The possibility that IPO valuations come in **below** the last private round — repricing the entire fund downward

**The shrewd investor prices this risk in** rather than being surprised by it.

The single most important rule: **Never pay more than 5-10% premium-to-NAV for any closed-end fund.** The history of DXYZ (500%+ premium, then -45%) is all the education you need.
