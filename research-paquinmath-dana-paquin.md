## 1. What Is PiMath?

**Paquin Institute of Mathematics** (PiMath, paquinmath.org) is a small, online, proof-based math enrichment program for advanced high school students (and exceptional middle schoolers), founded and solely taught by Dr. Dana Paquin.

- **Format:** Live online, seminar-style classes, twice per week (one lecture + one collaborative problem-solving/office-hours session)
- **Class size:** Capped at 12 students
- **Focus:** Proof-based mathematics, mathematical reasoning, exposition, and long-term mentorship — explicitly not contest-prep focused (though problems are at olympiad difficulty)
- **Financial aid:** Available; site explicitly states commitment to accessibility

### How PiMath Compares

| Program | Format | Focus | Duration | Class Size | Cost | Selectivity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PiMath** | Online, live | Proof, research mentorship | Academic year + summer | **12** | $800–$1,200/course | Application-based |
| Ross Program | Residential | Number theory, proofs | 6 weeks | ~60 total | ~$6,000 | Very high |
| PROMYS | Residential | Number theory, proofs | 6 weeks | ~80 total | ~$8,000 (free <$80K) | Very high |
| SUMaC | Residential/Online | Abstract algebra, topology | 3–4 weeks | ~80 total | ~$8,500 | Very high |

| AoPS Online | Online, async+live | Broad curriculum | Varies | 20–25+ | $400–$800/course | Open |

**PiMath's niche:** Much smaller than all competitors (12 vs 60–80+), year-round mentorship (not just summer), research pathway leading to actual publications, not contest-focused, more affordable than residential programs. Single instructor — strength (quality is verifiable) and constraint (no variety, limited scalability).

- Listed on AoPS Wiki's curated Summer Programs page

### What cannot be independently verified (yet)

- Specific outcomes of PiMath students (program is too new)
- The claim of "over one hundred" supervised research projects (plausible given ~18 years at Cal Poly + Stanford, but no independently verified count)
- Whether PiMath has any independent reviews/testimonials from parents or students

### What is notable in the negative

- **No independent third-party reviews of PiMath exist anywhere online** — no Trustpilot, Reddit, AoPS forum, or parent reviews
- PiMath has **no track record as an institution** — all credibility is Dr. Paquin's personal reputation
- She is **not a math olympiad competitor herself** (no USAMO/IMO mentions) — her expertise is in proof-based academic math, not competition math per se
- Publication rate (~14 papers over ~20 years) is **moderate**, consistent with a teaching-focused career
- **Single-person operation** — every course is taught by Dr. Paquin; no instructor variety, limited scalability, key-person risk
- AoPS Wiki page text is essentially identical to paquinmath.org — likely self-created, not organic community endorsement
- No published student outcomes, testimonials, sample materials, or syllabi beyond topic lists

### Best fit for

A student who wants deep, proof-based mathematical growth with close personal mentorship, research exposure, and a small cohort — and who is not primarily looking for contest preparation or a brand-name summer program for college applications.

### Worst fit for

A student looking for contest-prep (AMC/AIME/USAMO drilling), a large peer community, or a well-known program name that admissions officers will recognize.
